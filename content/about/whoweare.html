 What we do

The Speed Association of Computing Machinery is an University of Louisville registered student organization (RSO). The Speed ACM is a local university chapter of the ACM, the world's largest educational and scientific computing society. The ACM delivers resources that advance computing as a science and a profession, and we are dedicated to helping our fellow students advance their skills in today's computing world. All UofL students of any major are welcome to join and learn important skills and network with other computing professionals and students.

The UofL Chapter of the ACM provides many benefits to its members including:

  • Student Special Interest Groups
    • Cyber Defense
    • Game Development
    • Collegiate Cyber Defense Competition (CCDC)
    • Student Linux User Group (SLUG)
  • Labs on important computing topics
  • Hackathons
  • Networking and research opportunities with professors
  • Alumni Talks and profession development workshops
